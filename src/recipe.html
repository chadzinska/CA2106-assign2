Pizza Margherita

Prep time: 40min

Cook time: 18min

Serves: 4

Discover the soul of Italian cooking with this homemade Margherita pizza. Simple, vibrant, and bursting with flavor, this recipe celebrates the essence of Italy. Let's dive into the art of creating this classic dish right in your kitchen.

Ingredients

For the pizza dough:

  • 2 ¼ teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 1 ½ cups warm water (around 110°F or 43°C)
  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

For the toppings:

  • 3-4 ripe Roma tomatoes, thinly sliced
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

A Little About This Recipe

Crafting a Margherita pizza from scratch taught me more than just a recipe. It was a lesson in simplicity and patience. The process, from mixing the dough to slicing those ripe Roma tomatoes, revealed the beauty of handmade creations. Fresh mozzarella, as it melted over basil and tomatoes, highlighted the power of a few quality ingredients. As it baked, the pizza transformed, emerging from the oven golden and fragrant. That moment encapsulated the essence of cooking: understanding ingredients and allowing their flavors to shine. Making Margherita pizza isn't just following steps; it's embracing the wisdom of simplicity and letting flavors speak volumes. It's a reminder that sometimes, the most profound lessons come from the simplest recipes.

Instructions

1. Prepare the Dough:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the activated yeast mixture along with olive oil.
  3. Mix everything until it forms a dough. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, until it doubles in size.

2. Preparing the Pizza:

  1. Preheat your oven to its highest setting, typically around 500°F (260°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it preheats.
  2. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two portions. Roll out each portion into a round shape on a lightly floured surface, creating your pizza bases.
  3. Place the rolled-out dough onto a parchment-lined baking sheet or a pizza peel if using a pizza stone.
  4. Drizzle a bit of olive oil over the dough and sprinkle minced garlic on top.
  5. Arrange the tomato slices evenly over the dough, leaving some space around the edges for the crust.
  6. Add the slices of fresh mozzarella on top of the tomatoes.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Carefully slide the pizza onto the preheated pizza stone or place the baking sheet in the oven.
  9. Bake for about 10-15 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Once out of the oven, sprinkle fresh basil leaves over the hot pizza.
